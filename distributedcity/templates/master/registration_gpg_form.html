Step 2 of 3 - Specify OpenPGP Passphrase

Distributed City is built from the ground up as a secure community. It includes OpenPGP messaging (using GNU Privacy Guard*) for which you now need to choose a passphrase.

We will soon be introducing services which will allow you to communicate securely in our chat rooms, and communicate with other PGP users outside of Distributed City. In the meantime, you will use this passphrase to decrypt and read private messages sent to you by other Distributed City users.

Your passphrase must be longer than 8 characters in length, and may be composed of any alphanumeric or punctuation symbols. This passphrase will be used by you to decrypt and read your private messages. It is the key to your Distributed City identity.

Your passphrase is not stored in a readable form on the Distributed City servers. If you forget your passphrase, we cannot retrieve it for you, and you will be unable to decrypt any of your private messages.

* GNU Privacy Guard (GPG) is an open source OpenPGP compliant system for encrypting email and files.
